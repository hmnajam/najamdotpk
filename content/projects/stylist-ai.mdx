## Overview

> **Status: concept.** Stylist AI is a design I've sketched out but not yet built. What
> follows is the intended product, not a shipped one.

Every styling app I've seen eventually sells you clothes. Stylist AI starts from the
opposite premise: **you already own enough.** The interesting problem isn't what to buy,
it's what to wear from what's already in the cupboard.

## The intended design

- **Photograph your wardrobe once.** A vision pass catalogues each item — type, colour,
  cut, formality, season — so the system reasons about your actual clothes rather than a
  generic catalogue.
- **Dress for the occasion, not the aesthetic.** You'd say "client meeting, 38°C, outside
  half the day" and get outfits that respect the weather, the dress code, and what's
  clean.
- **It learns from what you reject.** The signal isn't the outfit you're shown, it's the
  one you actually wear. Preference gets inferred from behaviour rather than a
  questionnaire you fill in once and never revisit.
- **Gaps, not upsells.** If it repeatedly can't complete a look, it tells you which single
  item unlocks the most outfits — the one honest place a purchase suggestion earns its
  keep.

## Why it interests me

It's a good test case for agents operating on the physical world through a messy,
user-supplied dataset. Nothing about a real wardrobe is clean, labelled, or complete —
which makes it a more honest benchmark than a curated demo.
