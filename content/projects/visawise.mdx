## Overview

Visa consultants charge a lot for something that is, at its core, an information problem:
knowing which route fits your situation, what each one demands, and which part of your
file is going to get you refused. VisaWise turns that consultation into software.

## What it does

- **Routes you to the right visa.** A structured intake — purpose, nationality,
  destination, funds, ties, history — narrows dozens of possible categories to the ones
  you're actually eligible for, and says why the others were ruled out.
- **Builds the document checklist for your case.** Not the generic embassy list, but the
  one your specific route and circumstances require, in the order you should assemble it.
- **Finds the weak spot before the officer does.** Most refusals come down to the same
  handful of gaps — thin financials, unclear ties, an unexplained travel history. It
  flags those early, while there's still time to fix them.
- **Grounded in the published rules.** Requirements are retrieved from official sources
  rather than recalled from a model's memory. Immigration rules change, and a confident
  wrong answer here costs someone a filing fee and a season.

## Where it stands

The build is done to a working assistant; the live deployment is currently down while I
rework the rules corpus. Immigration guidance is only as good as how current its sources
are, and I'd rather have it offline than quietly stale.
